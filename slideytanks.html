  • Slidey Tanks is a top down shooter with a difference. Players use the recoil of their weapons to both destroy their opponent, and navigate around the map! This was a solo stage two university project, in which we were challenged with creating an original game mechanic and developing it into a working prototype. During the development I had the oppurtunity to adapt the game to function on an arcade machine, which is now used on University open days.

    Slidey Tanks was developed in Unity3D, originally for part of the AINT254 - Interactive Systems Module, a 12 week module at Plymouth University. ​ This project, and it's development, are used an example of stage two work for prospective students on the University website.

  • The Development Process

    Recoil-based Top Down Shooter

    The inspiration for the Slidey Tanks game mechanic originally came from the Tanks minigame from Wii Play, where players control a tank from a top down perceptive, and have to battle their way through 100 pre-made levels.My interpretation originally revolved around implementing a more complicated control system, involving a WiiMote. I realised early on that this was over the scope of the project, and most likely be more frustrating than enjoyable.

    I came to the conclusion that a great modern implementation of the top down shooter would use the the recoil as the way of movement. The tank would shoot, and the player would slide backwards. Initially a very simple idea, but as soon as I had a basic two player mode implemented, people were loving it. I saw potential for it a as a competitive couch coop game.

    I created prototype after prototype, tweaking the controls and adding various smaller mechanics such as charged shots and boost plates. Each time I added a new feature, I was able to test it on an audience at our Demo or Die sessions. After every session, I wrote a blog post keeping track of the results of my questionnaire, and how it would effect the coming weeks of development. I would really like to revisit Slidey Tanks in the future, and possibly start to gear it further towards mobile, as I believe this sort of title has more potential in the app market.
